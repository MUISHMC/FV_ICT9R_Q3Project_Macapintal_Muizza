Backstory

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye also known as "The Weeknd" was born February 16, 1990,
Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He is an RNB singer and songwriter.
Tesfaye’s mother and grandmother immigrated in the 1980s to Canada from Ethiopia,
and his first language was Amharic. He chose his stage name in tribute to when he was 17 years old, when, along with his friend La Mar Taylor, he dropped out of high school, took his mattress, and "left one weekend and never came home".

He grew up in the multicultural neighborhood of Scarborough.
In his youth, his mother worked multiple jobs to support the family, often as a nurse and nutritionist,
while he attended night school. His father later abandoned the family, leaving his maternal grandmother to take care of him. This allowed him to speak Amharic fluently and Semitic was his mother tongue. He also took her to an Ethiopian Orthodox service. Abel and his grandmother listened to Ethiopian music, which continued to influence the mysterious and sexy music he released.

In late 2010, Tesfaye anonymously uploaded several songs to YouTube under the name "The Weeknd".
He released three nine-song mixtapes in 2011: House of Balloons,
Thursday and Echoes of Silence to critical acclaim.
